Hello, I am a UX designer at Bloomberg LP, based in New York City. I work on framework and infrastructure design for the Bloomberg Terminal, focusing on discoverability, search, and consistency.

Before Bloomberg, I led design at OpenStudy, an education technology startup aiming to make the world one big study group.

I've showcased some of my work below. Please take a look, and contact me if you'd like to talk.

OpenStudy

As the only designer at ed-tech startup OpenStudy, I was responsible for user research, interaction design, visual design, and usability testing. In this case study, I briefly show how I led the OpenStudy user experience design from zero to over 150k users, from 2000 plus schools, spanning more than 150 countries.

Bloomberg Terminal

I work on infrastructure and framework design for the terminal. Some of my major projects include improving the command system, redesigning the search experience, and improving consistency across different applications on the terminal.

Please reach out to me if you’re curious, and I’d be happy to show you details.

Auditory Menus Research

As part of the Georgia Tech Sonification Lab‘s research team on Auditory Menus, I looked at ways to communicate the unavailability of menu items through sound.

We conducted multiple studies using a Java prototype, and analyzed results using repeated measures ANOVA and paired T-tests.
